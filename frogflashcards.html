Poison Dart Frog

Poison Dart Frog (aposematism)

Poison dart frogs are found in the humid, tropical rainforests of Central and South America, from Nicaragua down through the Amazon basin to Brazil, Peru, and Bolivia. Signaling potent skin toxins, derived from their insect diet, which can paralyze or kill predators and were historically used by indigenous peoples for blow darts.

it's stages are :- Egg ->   aquatic tadpole ->   adapted froglet ->   adult multicolour frog

Desert Rain Frog

Desert Rain Frog

The desert rain frog's specialities are its unique, squeaky defensive sound, terrestrial lifestyle in arid coasts (absorbing moisture from fog), waddling gait (no hopping), spade-like feet for burrowing backward, and direct development (no tadpole stage).

adapted to dry, sandy dunes in Namibia and South Africa

it's stages are :-egg   terrestrial tadpoles   terrestrial froglet   amphibian frog

Glass Frog

.Glass frogs' main specialty is their transparent belly, allowing you to see their heart, liver, and intestines, a unique trait among land vertebrates achieved by hiding red blood cells in their liver during the day.

found in Mexico Costa Rica, Panama. Ecuador, Colombia, Brazil, Venezuela, Bolivia, Peru, Argentina. Caribbean: Trinidad and Tobago

Wood Frog

Wood Frog

The Wood Frog's (Lithobates sylvatica) main speciality is its incredible ability to survive winter by freezing solid, with up to 65% of its body water turning to ice, .then thawing and reviving in spring,

found in North America, from Georgia and Alabama up US, across Canada , and into Alaska

it's stages are :- egg dark aquatic tadpole lung formed froglet adult heart freezing frog
